Onions have always been an essential part of the Philippine diet. AlamMoBa.net 19-Jan-2023

Onions have always been an essential part of the Philippine diet. From making the perfect sinigang soup to the most delicious adobo, onions are used in almost every dish. However, recently, onions in the Philippines have become more expensive than meat. This has caused a lot of consternation among Filipino households, as onions are a staple in many Filipino dishes.

The primary reason why onions have become more expensive in the Philippines is due to the supply and demand imbalance. The demand for onions has increased significantly in recent years, while the supply of onions has not been able to keep up with the demand. This has resulted in higher prices due to increased competition among buyers. Additionally, the high cost of labor, transportation, and storage has also contributed to the high cost of onions.

Apart from the supply and demand imbalance, there are also other factors that have contributed to the high price of onions in the Philippines. Weather is a big factor. Droughts, floods, and other extreme weather conditions can cause crop damage, resulting in reduced supply and higher prices. Additionally, the Philippines is a net importer of onions, so the fluctuation in international markets can also affect the prices of onions domestically.

The high cost of onions has had a major impact on the Filipino household. It has made meals more expensive as onions are a staple in most dishes. Additionally, it has also forced many households to reduce their consumption of onions, or switch to cheaper substitutes. This is especially problematic for those in the lower socio-economic brackets, who are already struggling to make ends meet.

MANILA, Philippines — Department of Education (DepEd). AlamMoBa.net 20-Jan-2023

MANILA, Philippines — Department of Education (DepEd) officials in the National Capital Region (NCR) and a teachers group have stressed the need to teach children about peace and mutual respect after a high school student was stabbed dead by his classmate at Culiat High School in Quezon City on Jan. 20.

“We are deeply saddened and disturbed that [a] violent incident such as this happened among our students inside the school which is supposed to be a safe place,” DepEd-NCR said in a recent statement as it offered assistance to the victim’s family.

“Follow-up stress debriefing sessions shall also be administered to students and teachers who witnessed the incident,” it added. “From the latest discussions, the plan is to just integrate that into the revised curriculum. It will be integrated but of course, nothing is final until we finish the study on it,” he said.

COVID-19 cases continue to fall. AlamMoBa.net 21-Jan-2023

MANILA, Philippines — Detected COVID-19 cases continued their downward trend, three years since the first coronavirus case was detected in the country. Over the past week, the number of people who tested positive for the virus every day fell by 35 percent, to a daily average of 270 from 419 the previous week.

The Department of Health (DOH) said out of the 1,891 new cases confirmed by laboratory tests, only one was in severe or critical condition. ccording to the DOH, 432 COVID-19 patients who are severely or critically ill are undergoing treatment in hospitals. There are currently 10,587 patients still infected with the virus. Of this total, 3,879, or 36 percent, are admitted to hospitals while the majority are presumed to be recovering at home. After three years of the pandemic, the DOH is still processing a huge backlog of reported COVID-19 deaths. The official death toll rose to 65,694 after the health agency confirmed 104 more deaths due to COVID-19, many of which occurred more than two years ago.

-Doona Z. Passibugan
